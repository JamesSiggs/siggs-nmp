📖Advisory Notes — Siggs & Co

pH is the master variable. At pH below 6.0, up to 30% of applied nutrient is unavailable to the crop. Liming is always the most cost-effective action on acid soils before any fertiliser investment — yet it remains one of the most neglected.

Organic matter drives N supply. Each 1% OM on a medium-textured soil mineralises approximately 20–30 kg N/ha/year under active biological conditions. High-OM farms with good biology demonstrably need less synthetic N.

Spring crops unlock the rotation. A spring crop after continuous autumn drilling reduces blackgrass pressure, allows overwinter soil biology to work, and typically needs 30–50 kg/ha less N than the equivalent autumn-sown crop. The economics made all-autumn rotations rational in the 1980s. The biology — and the blackgrass — tell a different story now.

Calcium:Magnesium ratio matters. A skewed Ca:Mg ratio — often from over-application of calcium limestone on already adequate soils — drives compaction tendency regardless of tillage. Diagnose before you lime.
